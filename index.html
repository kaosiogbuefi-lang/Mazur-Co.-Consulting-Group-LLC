The bottleneck

Scheduling is hard to balance

Scheduling managers juggle employee availability, peak business hours, and overtime limits. Because the process is mostly manual, it often relies on judgment and takes significant time to complete.

Demand changes hour-by-hour

Chick-fil-A traffic varies heavily by day and time — especially during lunch and dinner rushes. Fixed shift structures can create coverage gaps during busy periods and overstaffing during slow hours, reducing service speed and increasing labor cost.

AI solution

Our system replaces the “copy-paste schedule” approach with automated scheduling that reacts to projected demand. It generates a weekly schedule and a KPI summary report — without managers manually assigning every shift.

Optimization

Matches staffing levels to forecasted demand by hour to reduce gaps and unnecessary labor spend.

Time commitment

Automates shift assignments using availability + eligibility so managers spend less time scheduling.

Employee experience

Reduces scheduling errors (like assigning unavailable staff) and increases schedule reliability.

How it works

Inputs → AI pipeline → Outputs

Inputs

  • Hourly sales forecast (transactions / demand by hour)
  • Employee roster (FOH/BOH, eligibility)
  • Employee availability (who can work when)
  • Shift catalog (shift types the system can assign)

Outputs

  • Weekly schedule file (.TSV, convertible to Excel)
  • Summary report describing staffing + key KPIs

Schedule fields include: day, date, shift type, start time, end time, employee ID, employee name.

Stage 1

Translate hourly forecast into staffing needs (more transactions → more shift slots).

Stage 2

Filter eligible employees by department and availability (no one is scheduled when unavailable).

Stage 3

Assign shifts from the shift catalog and generate a clean schedule + KPI summary report.

KPIs we track

Coverage rate

Percent of hours meeting recommended staffing levels based on forecasted demand.

Schedule build time

Estimated manager time saved compared to manual scheduling.

Availability compliance

Percent of assigned shifts that match stated availability (goal: 100%).

Optional add-ons: overtime risk, labor cost per transaction, peak-hour service impact.
